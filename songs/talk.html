Writer(s): Andrew John Hozier-byrne
Hozier told about this song during the album release party, "There's all these lofty notions of, kind of, of true love, and lofty tales and kind of romantic language is used in the verses. And the chorus just admits to, like, 'I'm only using this language to distract from the fact that I'm trying to seduce you here, for fear that you find out how I'm imagining you.'"
This song has a reference to the ancient Greek myth of Orpheus and Eurydice, his wife, who died and whom he wanted to rescue from the Underworld, i.e. Hades.
