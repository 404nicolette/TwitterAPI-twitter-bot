Writer(s): Andrew John Hozier-byrne
"Be" has references to the Bible, e.g. Genesis, Saint Peter and the Gates of Heaven. Hozier explained the chorus in an interview with Entertainment, "It's about the myth of the Garden of Eden, about original sin. That myth is about two human beings being told what not to do and, in doing it, they perform an act of love, which upsets a greater power than themselves and they are cast out. I'm viewing their actions instead as liberating, and as a very necessary action. It's not so much a sin as a vital act of protest."
The line "When the gyre widens" may be a reference to William Butler Yeats's poem "The Second Coming".
When asked by a fan on Twitter about the line when he mentions Adam, Hozier answered, "Well. I figure that lad had an awful surprise on his hands is all."
